Documents You Need — Sort These First

Before applying anywhere

⚠️ Get These Sorted in Your First Weeks

Without the right documents you cannot legally work in Germany. Some of these take time to arrive by post — start early.

  • 🏠
    Anmeldung (Address Registration) — Do This First
    Register your address at the Bürgerbüro / Einwohnermeldeamt in Siegen. You need your passport, visa, and the rental contract signed by your landlord (Wohnungsgeberbestätigung). Everything else — your Steuer-ID, social security number — follows from this. Book an appointment at siegen.de
  • 🔢
    Steuer-ID (Tax Identification Number)
    An 11-digit number sent to your registered address automatically, usually within 2–4 weeks of Anmeldung. Every employer will ask for this. If it gets lost or delayed, you can request it again at: bzst.de
  • 🏥
    Krankenversicherungsnummer (Health Insurance Number)
    Your public health insurance card number from your Krankenkasse. Enrol with TK (Techniker Krankenkasse), AOK, Barmer, or DAK as soon as you arrive — this is mandatory in Germany. Employers need this number to register you on payroll. Your insurance card will show the number once issued.
  • 🆔
    Sozialversicherungsnummer (Social Security Number)
    Issued the first time you start a job with social insurance contributions. Your employer registers you for the first time. You can also request it proactively at drv.de if you need it before starting work.
  • 🎓
    Immatrikulationsbescheinigung (Enrolment Certificate)
    Download this from your Uni Siegen student portal every semester. Needed for Zenjob, staffing agencies, university jobs, and most student job applications. Always keep a fresh printed copy in your bag.
  • 🏦
    German Bank Account (IBAN)
    Required for salary payments. Open one at Sparkasse Siegen, Deutsche Bank, or a free online bank such as N26, DKB, or Commerzbank. Without a German IBAN, you cannot receive a German salary.
  • 📱
    German Phone Number
    Required on all job applications and CVs. Never use a foreign number on a German application — it reduces your chances significantly. Cheapest options: Aldi Talk or Lidl Connect for around €10 including credit. O2 and Telekom contracts are also available.

Work Rules — Know Your Limits

Non-EU students especially
Max hours during semester
20 hours per week — do not exceed this while lectures are running
Annual work limit (non-EU)
140 full days or 280 half-days per calendar year (updated March 2024)
Semester break
Full-time work (40h/week) is allowed during Semesterferien — use this wisely
Minijob monthly limit
Up to €603/month (as of Jan 2026) — no income tax, no social contributions
Minimum wage
€13.90 per hour (2025). Anything less is illegal — you can report it
HiWi / SHK at Uni
Student assistant roles at Uni Siegen do NOT count toward your 140-day annual limit
Self-employment
Non-EU students: not permitted without prior written approval from Ausländerbehörde
CV requirements
Always include: German phone number, Siegen address, degree name, university name

Job Apps — Easiest Way to Start

No CV needed · App-based shifts
Zenjob — Highly Recommended for International Students

What it is: Germany's biggest student job app. Register once, then book individual shifts directly in the app — no CV required, no interview, no application letters. One tap to confirm a shift. You receive 50% of your pay within days of finishing a shift.

Available roles: Warehouse, cashier, waiter/waitress, bar assistant, shelf stacking, event staff, office helper, promoter, delivery driver

Language: Many shifts available for English speakers. You complete a short language test in the app which unlocks suitable shifts.

Pay: €13.90–€16/hr on average. Paid quickly and reliably.

Coverage: Active in 40+ German cities. Siegen may have fewer listings than larger cities — expand your radius to include Dortmund, Cologne, or Wuppertal for more options, especially during semester breaks.

Important limit: As a short-term employee on Zenjob you can only work 70 working days per year through this contract type (separate from your 140-day visa limit). As a Werkstudent on Zenjob the standard 20h/week semester rule applies instead, which is more sustainable long-term.

What you need to register: Immatrikulationsbescheinigung + Steuer-ID + Sozialversicherungsnummer + German IBAN + valid passport or ID. Registration takes approximately one week — sign up early rather than waiting until you urgently need income.

Staffing Agencies in Siegen

Walk in with CV · Fastest route to local factory jobs
How Zeitarbeit (temp agency) work operates: You register with the agency, they place you at partner companies — local factories, DPD, logistics centres, supermarkets, Bison Polymers, and more. The agency is your legal employer, handles your payroll, tax, and contract. This is one of the fastest ways to get placed in Siegen. Walk in on a weekday morning with your documents and CV. Tell them: "Ich suche eine Stelle als Produktionshelfer oder Lagermitarbeiter, Teilzeit oder Minijob."

Walk-in or apply online · Near campus

Online & Remote Jobs

Work from home · Flexible schedule
Note for non-EU students: Self-employment and freelancing require prior written approval from the Ausländerbehörde. Working as an employee remotely — such as a Werkstudent contract or platform-based tutoring with a proper employment agreement — is generally fine. Always check your visa conditions if unsure.
  • 🎓
    HiWi / SHK Jobs at Uni Siegen — Academic assistant roles at the university. These do NOT count toward your 140-day annual work limit — making them the single best option alongside your studies. jobs.uni-siegen.de →
  • 📚
    Tutoring — Nachhilfe — Teach school subjects to local students. Platforms: Tutormatics.de (€15/45 min), Superprof.de, Nachhilfe.de, Schülerhilfe (has Siegen branches). Also post flyers on the university noticeboard — many local families prefer university students as tutors.
  • 🌍
    English Teaching Online — If you are a fluent English speaker. Lingoda offers proper employment contracts. AmazingTalker lets you set your own rate. Pay is typically €15–25/hr.
  • 🖥️
    Werkstudent IT / Tech (Remote) — If you study computer science, engineering, or business: search "Werkstudent remote" on LinkedIn, StepStone, and Indeed. Pay is typically €12–18/hr and it looks excellent on your CV.
  • 🔬
    Clickworker / Appen — Online data labeling, web research, and AI training tasks. Clickworker.com and Appen.com. Pay is variable but the work is flexible — good for filling one or two free hours between lectures.

Pro Tips & Walk-In Strategy

  • 📅
    The best time for walk-ins is Tuesday to Thursday between 2 and 4pm. Avoid Mondays (busy start of week), the lunch rush (12–2pm), and Friday evenings. Managers are most available and most relaxed during mid-afternoon weekdays.
  • 📄
    Always carry five to ten printed copies of your CV in German. Include your German phone number, Siegen address, degree name, and university. Even if a place says they are not hiring, leave a copy — it often leads to a call weeks later.
  • 🗣️
    The walk-in phrase that works: "Ich suche einen Minijob oder Nebenjob. Haben Sie gerade eine offene Stelle?" — straightforward and exactly what a manager needs to hear.
  • 🔄
    If they say no, ask: "Kann ich in zwei Wochen nochmal vorbeikommen?" — and then actually return. Consistent follow-up is one of the most effective things you can do and almost no one does it.
  • 🏢
    Staffing agencies are genuinely underused by students. Walking into two or three agencies from Section 4 with your documents can get you placed within days at a local company — often faster than any online application process.
  • ⚡
    Register on Zenjob while you are doing everything else. Verification takes about a week, but once active you can book extra shifts on any free day to supplement a main job without any additional applications.
  • ⚠️
    Red flags to watch for: No written contract offered, pay below €13.90/hr, pressure to exceed 20 hours per week during semester, or no Lohnzettel (payslip) provided. Report any of these to the Faire Integration advice centre for migrants.
  • 🎯
    Suggested priority order for your first week: Sort all documents → Register on Zenjob → Walk into two or three staffing agencies → Apply at BISON Polymers → Walk into nearby supermarkets → Apply via the DPD and Mensa online portals → Keep checking siegen.de/stellenangebote weekly.
